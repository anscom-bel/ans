Responsible Disclosure Policy

We value the work of security researchers and believe that fostering a collaborative relationship is key to improving our security posture. If you believe you have found a vulnerability in our systems, we encourage you to report it to us.

How to Report a Vulnerability

1. Our Promise

  • We will not pursue legal action against researchers who discover and report vulnerabilities in accordance with this policy.
  • We will acknowledge receipt of your report promptly.
  • We will provide an estimated timeline for remediation.
  • We will recognize your contribution publicly in our Hall of Fame, with your permission.

2. Your Guidelines

  • Do not disclose the vulnerability publicly until we have confirmed it is resolved.
  • Do not access, modify, or destroy any user data.
  • Do not use automated tools that may cause a denial-of-service (DoS) or degrade our services.
  • Do not use social engineering, phishing, or physical attacks against our employees or infrastructure.
  • Do provide a detailed report including a clear proof-of-concept.
  • Do provide your contact information so we can follow up.

3. Out of Scope

The following issues are generally considered out of scope for our program and should not be reported:

  • Missing or weak security headers (e.g., CSP, HSTS, etc.).
  • Brute-force attacks or missing rate-limiting on forms.
  • Vulnerabilities that require a user to install a malicious application.
  • Denial-of-Service (DoS) attacks.
  • Vulnerabilities in third-party services not under our direct control.

4. Hall of Fame

We are incredibly grateful for the security researchers who help us keep our systems secure. We will happily recognize your efforts on our public Hall of Fame page, with your permission.
